My Journey

From Electronics Engineering to Software Development

How I Got Here

My path into software wasn't exactly straight. I started with Electronics & Telecommunication Engineering in Mumbai, but quickly realized I was spending more time coding than working with circuits. That's when I knew software development was where I belonged.

The real learning happened at LTIMindtree, where I got thrown into building enterprise systems that needed to handle thousands of requests without breaking. There's nothing like production bugs at 2 AM to teach you about writing reliable code. Those 2+ years taught me that good software isn't just about making it work, it's about making it work when everything else is falling apart.

Co-founding FastFindFirm was a completely different beast. Going from zero to 5,000 users meant I had to think about scalability from day one. We built the whole platform with Node.js and Python, and watching it grow taught me as much about business as it did about technology.

Now at Northeastern, I'm diving deeper into the theory behind all the stuff I've been building. Distributed systems, machine learning, advanced algorithms. It's cool to finally understand the "why" behind some of the patterns I've been using.
